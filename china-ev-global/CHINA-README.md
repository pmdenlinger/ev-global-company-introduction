# China Introduction

Because the company was founded in Shenzhen, China and is a Chinese business entity, the company is regulated by the Chinese government, with the Ministry of Commerce and Trade playing a central role. The electric vehicle industry is now a major driver of the Chinese economy, and has become increasingly important overseas because of the quality and pricing of Chinese EVs.

Chinese EVs have become a major disruptive force for trade, and the US, under the Biden administration, introduced a 100% import duty on Chinese EV imports, just to cite one example.

Chinese EVs incorporate many Chinese materials and technologies which require custom export permits for manufacturing and export which require Ministry of Commerce and Trade approval on a case-by-case basis.

For EV-Global, this means that all offices need to be keenly aware of China policy developments which may affect them and their business.
