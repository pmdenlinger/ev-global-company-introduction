# EV-Global Company Introduction

EV-Global is a fictitious Chinese electric vehicle maker with design, engineering and manufacturing headquarters in Shenzhen, China. Its ex-China mainland PRC global headquarters are based in Hong Kong, which handles strategy, technology and sales outside of China. In addition to Hong Kong which functions as global headquarters, there are US, Canada, EU and Malaysia offices.

Each office has its own independent structure and management and subfolder in this repository, which includes company introduction, personnel list with titles, macro policies which affect the company, and records changes and events which affect each office.

Because China is the global leader in EV technology and manufacturing, the US, Canada and EU have trade and tech policies which affect EV-Global on key technologies, especially battery technology. The Chinese government considers these to be sensitive national technologies, and requires approval from the Ministry of Commerce before these technologies can be transferred outside China.

The company wants to expand its sales presence outside China, but it must comply with Chinese government policy, and follow local laws in each country where it has a presence.

Since there are geopolitical and trade tensions between China and G7 governments in the west, the management decided the best corporate structure would be for each office to have its own IT admin and compliance officer operating independently of China and Hong Kong management. The IT admin's job is to protect information security in each particular office, and the compliance officer's duty is to make sure that it follows the data laws and regulations of its government first. The company takes this very seriously; even visitors from the EV-Global who work in other offices are not given access to the local network, and are treated as outside personnel. This is to prevent data co-mingling and leakage which might be construed as a security compromise.

## EV-Global Purpose on Github

This company serves as an exhibit for Microsoft 365, Entra(identity management) and Purview (ediscovery, compliance and data governance) use cases which are hosted in related Github repositories. Each office has its own diretory/folder in this repository which includes separate files covering the company introduction, changelog which records company-specific events, macro-poliicy changes which affect the company, and personnel names and job titles. These use cases will reference scenario situations which happen at EV-Global, and will exhibit how these Microsoft tools, specifically Entra and Purview, are used in the handling of different situations.

Since Chinese data regulations require that Chinese corporations use Chinese applications and store data on Chinese servers in China, the Chinese company does not use Microsoft technologies except in Hong Kong, which has been the only exception in China for historical reasons from before 1997. This is why the Chinese company and its domestic data is not included in this repository, and it only references Hong Kong and other non-China offices.
